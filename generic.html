SELECTED ITEMS

The ten items we have chosen represent different aspects of Impressionism. Starting with sketches that never became full paintings and finishing with an educational podcast. Those items form an intricate relationship network and create a beautiful picture on their own without being outshined by the well known masterpieces. In choosing them we strived to present a less known side of the impressionism and to show that it can be not only about paintings.

  • The Carnegie Hall Library Of Classical Music

    "The Carnegie Hall Library of Classical Music: Impressionism 1900-1930" is a comprehensive collection that explores the rich musical landscape of the Impressionist period. Featuring works from 1900 to 1930, this anthology offers a curated selection of compositions that exemplify the innovative and evocative style of Impressionist music. From Debussy to Ravel, this collection provides listeners with a captivating journey through the soundscape of a transformative era in classical music history.

    Catalogue of the second exhibition

    The Catalogue of the Second Impressionism Exhibition is a valuable artifact documenting the vibrant artistic movement of the era. Printed in French, also this catalogue provides insight into the works showcased and the artists involved. It serves as a testament to the cultural significance of Impressionism and its enduring legacy in the art world.

    A bust of Renoir

    This sculpture portrays the renowned painter Renoir with a sense of intimacy and depth, showcasing Maillol's skill in conveying emotion through sculpted form. With meticulous attention to detail, Maillol immortalizes Renoir's likeness, inviting viewers to engage with the essence of the artist and his creative spirit.

  • A sketch made by Renoir

    DAS HUTANSTECKEN" (The Hatpinning) by Renoir is a captivating masterpiece that showcases the artist's mastery of capturing fleeting moments of everyday life. In this painting, Renoir skillfully depicts a scene of pinning hat, evoking a sense of quiet elegance and feminine grace. With vibrant colors and fluid brushstrokes, Renoir infuses the scene with warmth and intimacy, inviting viewers to immerse themselves in the charm and beauty of the moment.

    An essay about the movement

    In "Impressionism: Reflections and Perceptions," Meyer Schapiro offers a profound analysis of the Impressionist movement, highlighting its revolutionary techniques and lasting impact. Through insightful commentary, Schapiro invites readers to explore the significance of Impressionism in shaping modern art.

    A movie about the movement

    This flim offers a glimpse of relation between nature and Impressionist artworks, providing us with a deeper understanding of what "Impressionism" truly entails and how Impressionist artists accurately capture the essence of nature through their works. By examining the details and atmosphere of natural landscapes , we gain clearer insights into how these artists convey their unique perceptions and experiences of nature through color, light, and brushstrokes.

  • A photo of Monet's tomb

    The Tomb of Claude Monet, located in Giverny, France, serves as the final resting place of the legendary Impressionist painter. This serene and dignified memorial honors Monet's legacy and artistic contributions, attracting visitors from around the world to pay their respects. Surrounded by the tranquil beauty of nature, Monet's tomb stands as a timeless tribute to his enduring influence on the art world and his profound connection to the landscape that inspired his greatest masterpieces.

    A portrait of the artist Monet

    This captivating photograph, taken by an anonymous photographer, offers a rare glimpse into the life of the esteemed artist Claude Monet. The portrait captures Monet with a sense of authenticity, providing viewers with a window into the personality and essence of the master of Impressionism.

    "Claude Monet and the "Birth" of Impressionism

    Claude Monet painted "Impression: Sunrise" on November 13, 1872, marking the birth of Impressionism. This podcast episode delves into the painting's significance and discusses the validity of this claim.

  • Rose "Edgar Degas"

    The "Edgar Degas" rose is a cultivated variety of the Rosa genus, named after the renowned French artist Edgar Degas. Known for its pink and yellow hues, this rose was bred by Guy Delbard and is characterized by its association with the famous Impressionist painter.
