Around Impressionism - a Linked Open Data Project about the artistic movement is a project created for the course Knowledge Organization and Cultural Heritage of the Master Course Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge of the University of Bologna. The project highlights some milestones of one of the most influential periods in art history, as well as shows some items that are not coming to mind when we first think about Impressionism.

Impressionism emerged in the late 19th century as a revolutionary art movement, challenging traditional artistic conventions. Characterized by its emphasis on capturing fleeting moments and impressions of light and color, rather than precise detail, Impressionist painters sought to convey the essence of a scene through loose brushwork and vibrant palettes. Pioneered by artists such as Claude Monet, Edgar Degas, and Pierre-Auguste Renoir, Impressionism not only transformed the art world but also profoundly influenced modern art and continues to inspire artists and audiences worldwide.

About the project

In our project we attempted to represent data one way or another related to Impressionism and for that have chosen 10 specific items. The items we chose are not the usual, obvious ones (i.e. artworks) but rather sketches, studies, sculptures and even plants. Not the standard beautiful paintings of the epoch. Our goal, while choosing the items was to differentiate the habitual perception of the movement and to provide the spectators with something they might have not seen before. Still, just like the artworks could have done, the chosen items create a unique and distinguished picture of Impressionism.
